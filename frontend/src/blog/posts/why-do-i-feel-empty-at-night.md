## It's 2am and you feel nothing. Or everything. You're not sure which.

You're lying in bed and there's this hollow feeling in your chest. Not sadness exactly. Not anxiety exactly. Just... empty.

Like something is missing but you can't name what.

If this sounds familiar, you're not broken. You're not dramatic. And you're definitely not alone.

## What the emptiness actually is

The feeling most people describe as "emptiness at night" is actually your nervous system coming down from a full day of performing.

During the day, you're busy. Studying, socialising, being the version of yourself that other people see. That performance takes energy — and it also keeps the harder feelings at bay.

At night, when the distraction stops, everything you didn't have time to feel during the day surfaces.

The emptiness isn't nothing. It's everything you've been postponing.

## Why it feels worse at night specifically

Three things happen at night that don't happen during the day:

**The noise stops.** You don't have notifications, conversations, or tasks to focus on. Your brain finally has space — and it fills that space with everything you've been avoiding.

**Cortisol drops.** Your stress hormone naturally decreases at night, which can make you feel deflated after a day of running on it.

**You're alone with your thoughts.** And if your thoughts have been running on "I'm fine" all day, being alone with them feels like suddenly seeing behind a curtain.

## What it might be pointing to

The hollow feeling at night is often a signal, not a flaw:

**Loneliness you haven't acknowledged.** Not the obvious kind — the kind where you're surrounded by people but still feel unseen.

**Emotional avoidance.** If you stay busy, stay productive, stay "fine" — the night is when your actual feelings catch up.

**Disconnection from yourself.** Sometimes the emptiness is what it feels like to have performed a version of yourself all day that doesn't quite fit.

## What actually helps

**Name it out loud.** Actually say or type the specific feeling. "I feel hollow and I don't know why." Something about externalising it reduces its power.

**Don't try to fix it immediately.** The instinct is to grab your phone, scroll, distract yourself. That works short-term but keeps the cycle going.

**Ask yourself one question:** What did I not let myself feel today?
